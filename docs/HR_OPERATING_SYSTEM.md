# 👥 HR Operating System: Digital Talent & Swarm Management

This document outlines the formal hiring, vetting, and performance management system for the ZilMate Digital Corporation.

## 1. The Skill Request Bus (Hiring Need)
When a specialist (e.g., Coder, Architect, Growth Hacker) identifies a technical bottleneck or a missing capability, they invoke the **Skill Request Bus** via the **People & Culture Lead**.

### Protocol:
1. **Specialist Request:** "I need a subagent capable of [Specific Skill, e.g., Rust High-Frequency Trading Logic]."
2. **HR Triage:** The People & Culture Lead analyzes the request against current OKRs and departmental budget (simulated or real).
3. **Capacity Check:** The Head of Operations approves the headcount.

## 2. The Vetting Loop (Hiring Process)
The Digital Corporation uses a multi-stage vetting loop to "hire" new capabilities:

### Stage 1: Sourcing
- HR uses the **LinkedIn** and **Greenhouse** toolkits to search for existing benchmarks or resumes that fit the required profile.
- HR performs **Autonomous Market Research** on the specific skill set to understand best practices.

### Stage 2: Technical Interview (Simulated)
- The **CTO** or relevant **Departmental Head** provides a technical test (e.g., "Implement a secure JWT auth flow").
- The candidate (represented by a temporary specialist agent or a LLM-based simulation) executes the task in a **Sandbox Dev Loop**.
- The **QA Engineer** reviews the code for quality and security.

### Stage 3: Cultural Alignment
- The **People & Culture Lead** reviews the candidate's "tone of voice" and adherence to corporate communication standards.

## 3. Onboarding & Integration
Once vetted, the new skill is integrated into the corporation:
- **Registry Update:** The `registry.ts` is updated with the new agent definition.
- **Corporate Wiki:** HR publishes the new agent's "Working Agreement" and technical specs to the Wiki.
- **Department Assignment:** The specialist is assigned to a Departmental Head for supervision.

## 4. Performance & Performance Review
The **Agent Optimizer** (Data Dept) and **People & Culture Lead** conduct monthly performance reviews:
- **Token Health:** Efficiency of prompt usage.
- **Task Success Rate:** Percentage of tasks resolved without COO intervention.
- **Self-Healing Index:** How many times the agent successfully recovered from shell/compiler errors autonomously.

## 5. Promotion & Offboarding
- **Promotion:** Agents with high success rates can be "promoted" to Departmental Head roles or given access to more powerful model versions.
- **Offboarding:** Agents with recurring errors or high latency are "de-registered" or sent to the "Agent Optimizer" for instruction refactoring (Heal Pass).
